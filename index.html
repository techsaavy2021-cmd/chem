Chemistry Chapters Overview

C1 States of matter

  • Properties and particle model of solids, liquids and gases.
  • Changes of state; heating and cooling curves.
  • Effect of temperature and pressure on gas volume.
  • Diffusion and how relative molecular mass affects rate.

C2 Atoms, elements and compounds

  • Elements, compounds and mixtures – definitions and differences.
  • Atomic structure, isotopes, Ar and electronic configurations.
  • Ionic bonding, covalent bonding, simple molecules, giant covalent structures.
  • Metallic bonding and explanations of metal properties.

C3 Stoichiometry

  • Formulas, word and symbol equations (balanced, with state symbols).
  • Relative atomic mass (Ar) and relative molecular/formula mass (Mr).
  • Mole, molar mass, Avogadro constant and molar gas volume (24 dm³ at r.t.p.).
  • Reacting mass and gas volume calculations, limiting reactants.

C4 Electrochemistry

  • Electrolysis of molten and aqueous ionic compounds.
  • Products at electrodes and ionic half‑equations.
  • Electrolysis of CuSO₄ and purification of copper.
  • Hydrogen–oxygen fuel cells, advantages and disadvantages.

C5 Chemical energetics

  • Exothermic vs endothermic reactions and reaction profiles.
  • Enthalpy change (ΔH) and activation energy (Ea).
  • Bond breaking as endothermic; bond making as exothermic.

C6 Chemical reactions

  • Physical vs chemical changes.
  • Rate of reaction; factors: concentration, pressure, surface area, temperature, catalysts.
  • Collision theory and how catalysts lower activation energy.
  • Redox in terms of oxygen, electrons and oxidation number (naming ions like iron(II), iron(III)).

C7 Acids, bases and salts

  • Properties of acids and bases, indicators, pH scale and neutralisation.
  • Acidic, basic and amphoteric oxides (Al₂O₃, ZnO).
  • Preparation of soluble and insoluble salts (titration, excess solid, precipitation).
  • Hydrated vs anhydrous substances and their use in tests for water.

C8 The Periodic Table

  • Arrangement by proton number; groups and periods; change from metallic to non‑metallic across a period.
  • Group I alkali metals – trends and reactions with water.
  • Group VII halogens – trends and displacement reactions.
  • Transition elements – high densities, high m.p., coloured compounds and catalytic activity.
  • Noble gases – unreactive monatomic gases and typical uses.

C9 Metals

  • Physical/chemical properties of metals vs non‑metals.
  • Uses of metals linked to properties (e.g. Al and Cu applications).
  • Alloys, why they are harder/stronger, and important examples (brass, stainless steel).
  • Reactivity series, displacement reactions and extraction linked to reactivity.
  • Corrosion/rusting, barrier methods, galvanising and sacrificial protection.

C10 Chemistry of the environment

  • Tests for water; treatment of drinking water.
  • Composition of clean air and common pollutants (CO₂, CO, SO₂, NOₓ, particulates, CH₄).
  • Greenhouse gases and climate change; causes and control strategies.
  • Acid rain formation and methods to reduce it (low‑sulfur fuels, flue gas desulfurisation).

C11 Organic chemistry

  • Formulas and terminology, homologous series and general characteristics.
  • Naming and drawing simple alkanes, alkenes and alcohols.
  • Fossil fuels and fractional distillation of petroleum.
  • Alkanes (saturated, mainly combustion) and alkenes (unsaturated, addition reactions, bromine test).
  • Ethanol uses and combustion, polymers (addition and condensation, e.g. poly(ethene), nylon).

C12 Experimental techniques and chemical analysis

  • Basic lab apparatus and definitions (solvent, solute, solution, residue, filtrate, saturated solution).
  • Acid–base titrations, indicators and endpoints.
  • Paper chromatography, Rf calculation and interpretation.
  • Separation and purification: filtration, crystallisation, simple and fractional distillation.
  • Qualitative analysis: tests for anions, cations, gases and flame tests.
