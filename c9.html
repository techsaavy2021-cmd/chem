C9.1 Properties of metals

Metals vs Non-metals:

Thermal conductivity: Metals = good, Non-metals = poor
→ Due to delocalised electrons transferring energy

Electrical conductivity: Metals = good, Non-metals = poor

Malleability: Metals can be hammered into shape
→ Layers of ions slide over each other

Ductility: Metals can be drawn into wires

Melting/boiling points: Metals = high, Non-metals = low
→ Strong metallic bonding

Exceptions:
– Alkali metals → low melting points
– Mercury → liquid
– Graphite → conducts electricity
– Carbon & silicon → high melting points

Metallic bonding:
Giant lattice of positive ions in a sea of delocalised electrons.
Strong electrostatic attraction → requires lots of energy to break.

Reactions:
Metal + acid → salt + hydrogen
Metal + water → metal hydroxide + hydrogen
Metal + steam → metal oxide + hydrogen

C9.2 Uses of metals

Aluminium:
– Aircraft → low density
– Cables → good conductivity
– Food containers → corrosion resistant

Copper:
– Electrical wiring → excellent conductivity

C9.3 Alloys

Alloy: mixture of a metal with other elements

Examples:
– Brass = copper + zinc
– Stainless steel = iron + chromium + nickel + carbon

Why stronger?
Different atom sizes distort layers → harder for layers to slide → stronger and harder

C9.5 Corrosion

Rusting requires oxygen + water

Prevention:
– Painting / oiling
– Galvanising (zinc coating)
– Sacrificial protection

C9.6 Extraction of metals

High reactivity: electrolysis
Medium: reduction with carbon
Low: found native

Blast furnace:
Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂

Limestone:
CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂
CaO + SiO₂ → slag

Aluminium:
Extracted by electrolysis from bauxite
